Know before you book

What the demo is actually like

Here’s the whole thing, start to finish — so you can decide if it’s worth your time before you grab a slot.

The first five minutes

We say hi, and I ask what brought you here — your kitchen, a gift, or Cutco you already own. That decides everything I show you.

What I ask

How you cook, who cooks with you, what annoys you in the kitchen right now, and what you already have. No forms, just a conversation.

What you see

The main pieces in action — the rope cut, tomatoes, and the tools that match how you actually cook. In person you try them yourself; on video I demo up close.

How choosing works

I narrow it to a shortlist that fits your situation — usually one to three pieces, sometimes a set, sometimes “keep what you have.” You compare, I answer.

How pricing is handled

Site prices are a June 2026 snapshot. In the demo we confirm current pricing straight from Cutco — the same numbers published on cutco.com. No invented discounts.

If you buy nothing

Then you buy nothing, and that’s a fine outcome. You’ll still leave knowing what fits your kitchen, and we part as neighbors.

What happens after

If you do order, I handle it with Cutco and keep you posted to your doorstep. Either way I stay your contact for sharpening, service, and questions — for life.

My promise to you

Honest help. Your call.

If it's not right for you, that's completely fine — I'd rather earn your trust than sell you something you won't use. Lots of folks just want to see the cutlery and ask questions. That's genuinely welcome.

We can meet in person (Winnetka, Northbrook, Glencoe, Evanston, DePauw area) or hop on Google Meet or Zoom — your call, whatever's easier.
